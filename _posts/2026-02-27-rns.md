To bridge the performance gap between zero-shot and fully supervised image segmentation, we introduces a few-shot approach that combines text prompts with a small set of annotated reference images. By using a retrieval-augmented test-time adapter to learn the best fusion of these textual and visual features per query, the method significantly improves segmentation accuracy while maintaining open-vocabulary flexibility.
